Day 1: The OSI Model Fundamentals

Today I started my 90-day challenge by diving into the OSI (Open Systems Interconnection) model. In cybersecurity, this is our "map" for troubleshooting and defense.

 Key Takeaways:
- Layer 1-4: These are the "Lower Layers" where the technical networking happens (cables, IP addresses, and TCP handshakes).
- Layer 5-7: These are the "Upper Layers" where the user interacts with applications like web browsers or email.

Why it matters for a SOC Analyst:
Understanding these layers allows me to pinpoint where an attack is occurring. If I see a SYN Flood, I know I'm looking at a Layer 4 (Transport) issue.

Active Recall:
- Q: Which layer handles IP addresses and routing?
- A: Layer 3 (The Network Layer).
